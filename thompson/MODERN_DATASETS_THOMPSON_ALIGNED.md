# Modern Datasets Aligned with D'Arcy Thompson-Style Work

This is a curated shortlist of modern, pre-existing external datasets that map directly to Thompson-style transformation and allometry analyses.

## Selection Principles
- Public and citable (DOI preferred)
- Landmark-rich (2D/3D coordinates)
- Contains metadata for growth, taxa, or ecology where possible
- Reproducible ingestion (structured files such as TPS/CSV)

---

## Recommended Primary Datasets

## 1) Crocodylian cranial ontogeny (Dryad)
- **Dataset:** The utility of cranial ontogeny for phylogenetic inference (geometric morphometrics)
- **DOI:** `10.5061/dryad.14fn1`
- **Why Thompson-aligned:** strong allometric trajectories and shape transformation through growth.
- **Best visuals:**
  - age-stage trajectory morphospace,
  - TPS transformation grids between growth stages,
  - pairwise species transformation atlas.

## 2) Threespine stickleback allometry/plasticity (Dryad)
- **Dataset:** Ancestral plasticity and allometry in threespine stickleback
- **DOI:** `10.5061/dryad.hb824gd4`
- **Why Thompson-aligned:** fish forms plus allometry gives direct continuity with classical fish transformation examples.
- **Best visuals:**
  - classical fish-to-fish transformation grids,
  - ecotype trajectory shifts in morphospace,
  - affine vs TPS residual comparison panels.

## 3) Gambusia predation-convergence morphometrics (Dryad)
- **Dataset:** Predation drives morphological convergence in Gambusia panuco group
- **DOI:** `10.5061/dryad.j7n42`
- **Why Thompson-aligned:** environment-linked transformations of form support geometry-first comparative panels.
- **Best visuals:**
  - convergence maps in morphospace,
  - deformation-grid comparisons across habitat/predation regimes.

## Strong Secondary Datasets

## 4) Facial growth trajectories (Dryad)
- **Dataset:** Facial growth and development trajectories based on 3D images
- **DOI:** `10.5061/dryad.6m905qg5w`
- **Why useful:** large sample size and rich developmental trajectory data.
- **Caveat:** domain is human craniofacial growth (less directly tied to fish examples, but excellent for growth-form analysis).

## 5) Anolis limb allometry (Dryad)
- **Dataset:** Conserved ontogenetic limb allometry in Anolis
- **DOI:** `10.5061/dryad.jsxksn08d`
- **Why useful:** clean allometric signal, good for Thompsonian growth laws framing.

## Practical MVP Recommendation

If you want a Thompson-modern MVP now:
1. Start with **Dryad 10.5061/dryad.14fn1** (crocodylian ontogeny) for trajectory + grids.
2. Add **Dryad 10.5061/dryad.hb824gd4** (stickleback) for fish-based transformation panels.

This gives one growth-centric and one fish-centric dataset, both modern and externally sourced.
